MDMA SAFETY GUIDELINES

What is MDMA?

MDMA, also known as "ecstasy" or "molly," is a synthetic drug that alters mood and perception. It is widely known for promoting feelings of euphoria, empathy, and energy, but its use requires caution due to potential health risks.

Usage Recommendations

  • Always start with a small dose, especially if it's your first time.
  • Avoid mixing MDMA with alcohol or other substances. The combination can be dangerous.
  • Stay hydrated, but don't overdo it with water. Keep balanced.
  • Rest and avoid dancing for prolonged periods. Excessive heat can overload your body.

Effects on the Body

MDMA use causes an increase in serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine levels, which are neurotransmitters affecting mood, pleasure, and stress. The euphoric effect can last 3 to 6 hours, but this varies based on dosage and individual metabolism.

Risks of Use

Despite short-term pleasant effects, MDMA use can cause dehydration, increased blood pressure, anxiety, and depression in the following days. Excessive and continuous use can damage the brain's neurotransmitters, causing memory problems, confusion, and mood disorders.

Precautions and Prevention

  • Avoid using MDMA if you are taking prescription medications or have a history of heart disease, high blood pressure, or psychiatric problems.
  • Get adequate rest after use, allowing your body to recover.
  • Always consult a doctor if you experience any prolonged adverse side effects.
  • If you are at a party or event, make sure you are with trusted friends and know where to seek help if needed.

The Importance of Harm Reduction

The concept of "harm reduction" aims to minimize the risks associated with substance use. If you choose to use MDMA, always keep in mind that responsible use can reduce the negative impact on your physical and mental health.
